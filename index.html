Research

I'm interested in novel principled techniques for semi-automatically distilling structured knowledge from text and integrating it with existing structured knowledge bases.

Currently I'm exploring how to use semi-supervised variational autoencoders to learn a semantic parser by leveraging an existing knowledge base.

I'm broadly interested in machine learning, natural language understanding, information extraction and semantic parsing, knowledge discovery and representation, probabilistic graphical models, and representation/deep learning.

Projects

PyTorch Monitor

PyTorch Monitor is a simple python package that builds on tensorboard and tensorboard-pytorch to promote good practice in deep learning experiments. It makes experimental setups reproducible and automatically inspects and monitors models during training to make debugging (IMHO, about 60% of deep learning) dead simple.

Detecting Foodborne Illness on Social Media

Joint work with Lampros Flokas, Yogesh Garg, and Anna Lawson

Advised by Luis Gravano and Daniel Hsu

Many people disclose incidents foodborne illness on Yelp and Twitter. In collaboration with the NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene, we built a document classification system that mines Yelp and Twitter for reports of foodborne illness to facilitate the targeted investigation of foodborne illness outbreaks.

The Posts Recommendation Algorithm for dExplorer

Joint work with Drashko Nakikj

Using a mixture of unsupervised document similarity methods, we recommend relevant posts in medical discussion forums to help users find the information they're seeking faster. We're currently developing a chrome extension to put the algorithm into practice.

FindIt: Smart Web Crawling

Advised by Bina Ramamurthy

Often different websites host many pages that share a common theme, but have non-uniform structure, such as college course descriptions or product catalogs.

FindIt uses semi-supervised machine learning to quickly learn search strategies that generalize across website organizational structures.

Fingerprints as Passwords

Joint work with Matt Morse, Jesse Hartloff, and more. See publications.

Advised by Atri Rudra

This project was at the intersection of cryptography, biometrics, and pattern recognition.

The goal was to develop theoretically secure encryptions of fingerprint templates such that matching was still possible in the encrypted space.

iGlobe: Geospatial Machine Learning

Advised by Varun Chandola

iGlobe is a machine learning layer built on top of NASA's WorldWind. It allows for the incorporation, visualization, and analysis of independent geospatial datasets.

I worked on this project with my machine learning professor, Dr. Varun Chandola for the NASA Europa Challenge, where our team won 2nd place for university entries.
